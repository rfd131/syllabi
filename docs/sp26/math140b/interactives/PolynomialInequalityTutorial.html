How to read a sign chart
The method, end-to-end.

Step 1 · List the zeros

Each factor (x − r)m gives a zero at x = r. The exponent m is the multiplicity; it controls whether the product changes sign when x passes through r.

Step 2 · Read off each factor\'s sign on each interval

Between two adjacent zeros, each factor has a constant sign. A linear factor (x − r) is negative to the left of r and positive to the right. Its square (x − r)² is never negative: the square of a real number is always ≥ 0.

Step 3 · Multiply signs to get the product sign

Signs multiply like signs: (−)(−) = (+), (−)(+) = (−), and so on. An even-multiplicity factor contributes (+) on both sides of its root. An odd-multiplicity factor flips the product sign when you cross its root.

Step 4 · Pick the intervals that satisfy the inequality

For P(x) > 0, pick the intervals where the product row shows (+). For P(x) ≥ 0, also include each root (the equation is satisfied there because P(root) = 0). Strict inequalities use parentheses; non-strict use brackets. Adjacent intervals separated by an included root merge into one bigger interval.
