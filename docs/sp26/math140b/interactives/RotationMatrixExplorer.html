    The geometry of complex eigenvalues. A real 2×2 matrix of the form r·R(θ) rotates every vector by the angle θ while scaling its length by r. The eigenvalues are the complex conjugate pair λ = r·e±iθ = r·cos θ ± r·sin θ·i. Their modulus is exactly r, and their argument is exactly ±θ.

    Three regimes. When r = 1 the orbit traces a circle — a pure rotation, energy conserved, population cycling. When r < 1 the orbit spirals inward toward the origin (damped oscillation). When r > 1 the orbit spirals outward (amplified oscillation). Watch the eigenvalue points cross the unit circle as you drag r.

    Period vs. quasiperiod. When θ is a rational multiple of π the orbit is periodic — for θ = π/6 the period is 12 (since 12·(π/6) = 2π). Irrational θ produces a quasiperiodic orbit that never exactly closes.
