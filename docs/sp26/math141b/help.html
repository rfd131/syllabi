Getting Help

Getting Help in MATH 141B

Success in MATH 141B comes from actively engaging with the material and seeking help when needed. We offer multiple resources to support your learning throughout the semester. Don't wait to get help.

Remember: For the first 9 foundational learning targets (LT1F-LT9F), if you don't earn a Success during the first two attempts, you'll need to visit an LA or instructor office hour before attempting again. This requirement helps ensure you get the support needed to succeed.

Instructor Office Hours

Office hours are a time for one-on-one help. You can review course material, get clarification about quiz feedback, or discuss concerns about the course. Office hours are "drop-in". No appointment is needed for scheduled office hours.

LA Community Learning Sessions

Structured Group Learning with Learning Assistants

Learning Assistants run community learning sessions most weeks during the semester. These are structured problem-solving sessions where students work in groups reviewing recently covered class material.

What to Expect:

  • Work in small groups with guidance and support from Course LAs
  • Practice and review concepts and skills from recent classes
  • Work on practice quizzes in preparation for upcoming quiz sessions
  • Form peer study groups that extend beyond the classroom and LA Sessions
  • Participation in LA Sessions earns 20 XP

Times and Locations: TBD

Complete schedule and room locations will be posted on Canvas at the beginning of the semester.

Earn XP: Participation in LA Community Learning Sessions earns 20 Experience Points per week, contributing to potential grade modifications.

Guided Study Groups (GSG Sessions)

Peer-Led Study Sessions

Guided Study Groups are peer-facilitated study sessions designed to help you master course content through collaborative learning. These sessions are led by undergraduate students who have previously excelled in MATH 141B.

What GSG Sessions Offer:

  • Review of key concepts from recent lectures
  • Practice problems similar to Learning Target Quizzes
  • Study strategies specific to MATH 141B
  • A supportive environment to ask questions
  • Opportunity to learn from peers who recently succeeded in the course

Schedule Information:

GSG sessions meet once per week. Information about our GSG sessions will be announced on Canvas during the first week of classes.

GSG sessions are free and open to all MATH 141B students. No registration required - just show up!

The Math Center @ Penn State Learning (Free Drop-in Tutoring)

Drop-in Tutoring at 7 Sparks Building

The Math Center offers free drop-in tutoring where students work individually or in small groups with undergraduate peer tutors who have excelled in MATH 141B. Sessions are designed to address specific needs including homework help, quiz and exam preparation, and understanding particular concepts.

What to Expect:

  • Session Length: Typically up to 30 minutes per student if there's a wait
  • Location: 7 Sparks Building
  • Format: Individual or small group tutoring
  • Come Prepared: Bring questions and course materials

How Tutors Help:

  • Clarify course concepts with alternative explanations
  • Work through similar practice problems
  • Review material from the week
  • Address prerequisite knowledge gaps
  • Help with quiz and exam preparation

Note: Tutors do not provide direct answers to graded homework assignments but will help you practice similar problems to build understanding.

High Demand: The Math Center is usually very busy during fall semester. Consider also attending GSG sessions for weekly review. All Penn State Learning services are included in tuition and free to access as often as needed.

For more information, visit: pennstatelearning.psu.edu/tutoring/mathematics

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Help Resources

  • Start early: Don't wait to seek help.
  • Be consistent: Regular in-class attendance and participation, consistent participation in LA Community Learning Sessions, and consistently working in study groups with your peers are more effective than cramming.
  • Form study groups: Connect with classmates during class and in LA sessions or GSG sessions.
  • Review quiz feedback: Prepare for office hours by reviewing previous quiz attempts and preparing questions.
  • Ask questions in class: Your instructors want to help - don't be shy about asking for clarification.
