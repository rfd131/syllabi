Checklist: reading f′ off the graph of f
A short reference to keep in view while you sketch.

Sign of f′

Where f is increasing, f′ is positive (dots sit above zero).
Where f is decreasing, f′ is negative (dots sit below zero).

Zeros of f′

Where f has a horizontal tangent — a local max, a local min, or a horizontal inflection — f′ = 0.
Passing from + to − marks a local max of f; from − to + marks a local min.

Size of f′

Where f is steep, |f′| is large.
Where f is flat-ish, f′ is near zero.

Bending of f

Where f is concave up, f′ is increasing.
Where f is concave down, f′ is decreasing.
