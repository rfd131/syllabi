    Reading the spectrum. For a non-negative matrix, the Perron–Frobenius theorem guarantees a real non-negative eigenvalue λPF with modulus at least as large as every other eigenvalue. This dominant eigenvalue sets the long-term geometric growth rate: if λPF > 1 the population grows, if < 1 it declines.

    Complex eigenvalues come in conjugate pairs and produce oscillations. Their modulus |λ| governs whether the oscillation grows (|λ| > 1), decays (|λ| < 1), or persists indefinitely (|λ| = 1). Try the grasshopper preset to see three eigenvalues with |λ| = 1 — populations cycle forever with period 3 and never converge to a stable distribution. The succession presets, by contrast, are column-stochastic Markov chains: total mass is conserved (λPF = 1 exactly), and the proportions view shows convergence to a stationary distribution.
