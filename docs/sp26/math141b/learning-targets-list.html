LT1 Two-Part Review topics from Calculus I ▼
Foundational (LT1F)
"Demonstrate proficiency in computing definite integrals using antiderivatives, signed area, and properties of the definite integral."
Learning Objectives:
  • Use Evaluation Theorem for integral computation
  • Apply antiderivative rules correctly
  • Compute integrals using signed area
  • Interpret net change in applications
Advanced (LT1Adv)
"Demonstrate proficiency in conceptual understanding of the definite integral and its applications."
Learning Objectives:
  • Distinguish definite integrals as numbers vs. indefinite as families
  • Determine integral units in applications
  • Construct definite integrals from verbal descriptions
  • Understand integral properties over intervals
LT2 Two-Part Integration by Substitution ▼
Foundational (LT2F)
"Demonstrate proficiency in performing integration by substitution."
Learning Objectives:
  • Understand substitution-chain rule relationship
  • Use substitution for indefinite integrals
  • Identify appropriate substitutions
Advanced (LT2Adv)
"Apply substitution method to definite integrals with proper change of variables."
Learning Objectives:
  • Make variable changes in definite integrals
  • Compute definite integrals via substitution
  • Combine substitution with other techniques
LT3 Two-Part Integration by Parts ▼
Foundational (LT3F)
"Use the integration by parts method to compute indefinite integrals."
Learning Objectives:
  • Apply integration by parts formula
  • Choose appropriate u and dv
  • Recognize when integration by parts is appropriate
  • Demonstrate an understanding of the integration by parts rule and its connection with the product rule for derivatives
Advanced (LT3Adv)
"Use integration by parts to compute definite integrals and combine with other methods."
Learning Objectives:
  • Compute definite integrals using integration by parts
  • Combine integration by parts with other techniques
LT4 Two-Part Integration by Partial Fractions ▼
Foundational (LT4F)
"Perform partial fraction decomposition for rational functions."
Learning Objectives:
  • Decompose rational functions into partial fractions.
Advanced (LT4Adv)
"Compute integrals using partial fraction decomposition."
Learning Objectives:
  • Integrate rational functions using partial fractions
  • Recognize when partial fractions is appropriate
  • Combine with other integration techniques
LT5 One-Time Integration Techniques ▼
One-Time Proficiency (LT5)
"Demonstrate proficiency in identifying and applying appropriate integration techniques from LT1-LT4."
Learning Objectives:
  • Identify which integration technique to apply
  • Select between substitution, parts, and partial fractions
  • Combine multiple techniques as needed
  • Verify integration results by differentiation
LT6 Two-Part Modeling with Differential Equations ▼
Foundational (LT6F)
"Create differential equation models from verbal descriptions."
Learning Objectives:
  • Create differential equation models from verbal descriptions
  • Determine the order of a differential equation
  • Distinguish autonomous from non-autonomous equations
Advanced (LT6Adv)
"Verify solutions to differential equations."
Learning Objectives:
  • Verify solutions for first-order differential equations
  • Verify solutions for second-order differential equations
  • Determine particular solutions from initial conditions
LT7 Two-Part Separation of Variables ▼
Foundational (LT7F)
"Demonstrate proficiency in identifying separable first-order differential equations."
Learning Objectives:
  • Identify separable vs. non-separable equations
  • Separate variables algebraically
  • Find general solutions in implicit form
Advanced (LT7Adv)
"Solve separable differential equations and find particular solutions."
Learning Objectives:
  • Find explicit solutions when possible
  • Apply logarithm and exponentiation fluently
  • Find particular solutions from initial conditions
LT8 Two-Part The Integrating Factor Method ▼
Foundational (LT8F)
"Demonstrate understanding of the integrating factor method."
Learning Objectives:
  • Identify first-order linear differential equations
  • Convert to standard form
  • Compute the integrating factor
Advanced (LT8Adv)
"Apply the integrating factor method to solve differential equations."
Learning Objectives:
  • Understand connection to product rule derivative
  • Find general solutions using integrating factors
  • Find particular solutions from initial conditions
LT9 Two-Part Linear Models in Biology ▼
Foundational (LT9F)
"Construct differential equation models, find solutions, and interpret results in biological contexts."
Learning Objectives:
  • Construct differential equation models from biological scenarios
  • Select appropriate solution technique
  • Find general and particular solutions
  • Interpret solutions in biological context
LT10 Two-Part Slope Fields ▼
Foundational (LT10F)
"Demonstrate proficiency in constructing slope fields for first-order differential equations."
Learning Objectives:
  • Sketch slope fields for differential equations
  • Compute slopes at given points
  • Identify nullclines
Advanced (LT10Adv)
"Interpret slope fields and sketch solution curves."
Learning Objectives:
  • Sketch solution curves on slope fields
  • Recognize equilibrium solutions from slope fields
  • Analyze long-term behavior from slope fields
LT11 Two-Part Phase Lines and Equilibrium Solutions ▼
Foundational (LT11F)
"Demonstrate proficiency in constructing phase lines and identifying equilibrium solutions."
Learning Objectives:
  • Construct phase lines for autonomous equations
  • Determine equilibrium solutions
  • Identify stable, unstable, and semi-stable equilibria
Advanced (LT11Adv)
"Analyze equilibrium solutions and long-run behavior."
Learning Objectives:
  • Classify stability of equilibrium solutions
  • Verify equilibrium solutions algebraically
  • Interpret equilibrium solutions in biological contexts
  • Reason about long-run behavior across initial conditions
LT12 Group Bifurcation Diagrams ▼
Group Assessment (LT12)
"Demonstrate understanding of bifurcation diagrams and parameter effects on differential equation behavior."
Learning Objectives:
  • Sketch bifurcation diagrams
  • Identify bifurcation values
  • Describe qualitative behavior changes at bifurcations
  • Analyze parameter effects on long-run behavior
  • Understand tipping points and hysteresis

Group assessment combining graphical analysis with parameter interpretation.
